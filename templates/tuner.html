A tuner is an essential tool for musicians that helps ensure their instruments are accurately tuned to the correct pitch. By measuring the frequency of a played note and comparing it to a standard reference pitch, usually A4 at 440 Hz, a tuner can indicate if the note is too sharp (higher than desired) or too flat (lower than desired). This allows musicians to adjust the tension of their instrument's strings, reeds, or other components until each note matches the target pitch.


The standard tuning for a six-string guitar is as follows, with the corresponding note names and octaves:


1. 6th String: E2 (82.41 Hz)

2. 5th String: A2 (110.00 Hz)

3. 4th String: D3 (146.83 Hz)

4. 3rd String: G3 (196.00 Hz)

5. 2nd String: B3 (246.94 Hz)

6. 1st String: E4 (329.63 Hz)


This tuning, often called E standard tuning, is the most commonly used tuning in Western music for guitars. It allows for a balance of low and high notes, making it versatile for a wide range of genres. The tuning moves in intervals of perfect fourths between adjacent strings, except for the interval between the 2nd (B) and 3rd (G) strings, which is a major third.
